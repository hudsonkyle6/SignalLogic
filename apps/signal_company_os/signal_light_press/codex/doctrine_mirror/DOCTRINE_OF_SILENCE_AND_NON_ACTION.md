PURPOSE



This doctrine affirms that silence and non-action are valid, often superior system states.



Action is optional.

Integrity is not.







1\. SILENCE AS A FIRST-CLASS STATE



Silence indicates:

• insufficient certainty

• misaligned authority

• boundary proximity

• incomplete truth



Silence is not failure.

It is discipline.







2\. NON-ACTION CONDITIONS



Systems must choose non-action when:

• recovery is uncertain

• boundaries are unclear

• authority is absent

• incentives distort judgment







3\. PROHIBITIONS



No system may:

• speak to maintain relevance

• act to avoid appearing idle

• fill space with narrative







SEAL



Silence preserves survivability.

This doctrine is binding.
