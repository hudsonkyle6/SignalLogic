📜 ENGINE–WARDEN INTERFACE CANON

Signal Light Press — Canonical Governance Document

This document defines the lawful interfaces between the Engines (Oracle, Sage, Shepherd, Helmsman) and the Wardens (Sea, Hearth, Craft).
It ensures that intelligence may inform industry without becoming industry, and that industry may execute without governing itself.

DOCUMENT METADATA

Authority: Signal Light Press

Classification: CROWN JEWEL

Domain: Doctrine / Architecture

Status: Canonical

Applies To: All Engines, All Wardens, All Domains

Amendment Rule: Signal Light Press only

I. GOVERNING PRINCIPLE

Engines govern meaning and timing.
Wardens govern execution and craft.
No Engine may execute.
No Warden may interpret doctrine.

This separation is absolute.

II. ROLE OF THE WARDENS (NON-INTELLIGENT BY DESIGN)

The Wardens are industrial stewards, not cognitive authorities.

They exist to:

Build

Move

Store

Maintain

Operate

Respond

They do not exist to:

Decide strategy

Interpret signals

Redefine risk

Adjust doctrine

Optimize themselves

III. ENGINE → WARDEN INTERFACES (PERMITTED FLOWS)
1. ORACLE → WARDENS

Oracle MAY provide:

Environmental state (weather, season, temperature)

Volatility bands

Constraint flags (e.g., unsafe, unstable)

Drift indicators (non-prescriptive)

Integrity warnings

Oracle MAY NOT provide:

Instructions

Priority ordering

Urgency framing

Operational directives

Oracle reveals conditions. It does not tell the Wardens what to do.

2. SAGE → WARDENS

Sage MAY provide:

Posture (e.g., restraint, consolidation, steady execution)

Narrative context

Canonical constraints

Readiness framing

Cultural and seasonal meaning

Sage MAY NOT provide:

Tactical steps

Process changes

Scheduling commands

Performance targets

Sage gives meaning, not method.

3. SHEPHERD → WARDENS

Shepherd MAY provide:

Resource allocations

Timing windows

Budget envelopes

Supply priorities

Risk ceilings

Shepherd MAY NOT provide:

Operational tactics

Craft decisions

Safety overrides (unless contractual)

Doctrinal reinterpretation

Shepherd releases resources. Wardens decide how to use them.

4. HELMSMAN → WARDENS (INDIRECT ONLY)

Helmsman MAY influence Wardens only via:

Shepherd constraints

Sage posture

Long-arc narrative communicated by leadership

Helmsman MAY NOT:

Issue direct orders

Circumvent Shepherd allocation

Inject urgency into operations

The Helmsman steers the Company, not the shop floor.

IV. WARDEN → ENGINE INTERFACES (LIMITED)
WARDENS → ORACLE

Wardens MAY provide:

Operational telemetry

Environmental observations

Incident reports

Wardens MAY NOT:

Request reinterpretation

Influence signal weighting

Suppress measurements

WARDENS → SAGE

Wardens MAY provide:

Completion records

Incident narratives

Capacity feedback

Wardens MAY NOT:

Ask for posture changes

Negotiate doctrine

Pressure narrative framing

WARDENS → SHEPHERD

Wardens MAY provide:

Resource utilization

Constraint violations

Feasibility limits

Wardens MAY NOT:

Demand allocations

Self-authorize expansion

Cross domains without approval

V. HI (HARMONIC INTELLIGENCE) EMBEDDING RULE

Harmonic Intelligence (HI) is:

A methodology

A measurement framework

A control philosophy

HI is not:

An authority

An autonomous system

A decision-maker

A product with agency

HI lives within Wardens, not above them.

VI. CROSS-WARDEN PROHIBITIONS

Wardens MAY NOT:

Share control systems laterally without Shepherd mediation

Coordinate strategy independently

Create shadow intelligence loops

Merge domains without doctrine

Cross-warden coordination occurs only through Engines.

VII. FAILURE & SAFETY RULES

If Engine outputs conflict:

Wardens default to safe mode

Operations slow or pause

Human arbitration is required

If Warden execution violates posture:

Shepherd constrains resources

Sage documents deviation

No automated correction occurs

VIII. CANONICAL RULE

Industry expresses intelligence — it does not generate it.
