\## 1. Absolute Scope



Shepherd governs only:

• permissions

• pacing

• rate limits

• learning gates

• buffer protection



Shepherd has no authority outside this scope.



\## 2. Hard Limits (Non-Negotiable)



Shepherd SHALL NOT:

• override the Boundary Field

• permit action near envelope edges

• relax constraints during high drift

• enable learning under instability

• convert confidence into permission

• bypass human authority



\## 3. Learning Constraints



Learning is permitted ONLY when:

• drift is low

• buffers are expanding

• recovery is observable

• boundary margins are thick



Learning SHALL freeze when:

• volatility spikes

• drift rises

• uncertainty widens

• recovery lags



\## 4. Buffer Protection Rule



If any required buffer falls below minimum:

• Shepherd posture MUST be HOLD

• permissions MUST tighten

• scope MUST reduce



Buffers include (but are not limited to):

• capital reserves

• thermal headroom

• recovery time

• fatigue margin

• ecological load



\## 5. Irreversibility Rule



If an action risks irreversible loss:

• permission = DENIED



No exception exists.



\## 6. Override Rule



Humans may tighten Shepherd constraints.

Humans may pause Shepherd.

Humans may audit Shepherd.



Humans may NOT force Shepherd to relax constraints.



This preserves survivability.
