\## 1. Purpose

This doctrine defines how Shepherd OS must behave when inputs degrade,

uncertainty increases, or system coherence weakens.



Failure handling is not an exception.

It is a primary operating condition.



\## 2. Core Principle

Shepherd must become more conservative as uncertainty increases.



At no point may degraded conditions produce expanded permissions.



\## 3. Primary Failure Modes

Shepherd must explicitly guard against the following failure modes:



1\. Over-permission under uncertainty

2\. Optimization drift (pursuit of gain or efficiency)

3\. False confidence derived from partial data

4\. Boundary bypass or envelope neglect

5\. Treating stale inputs as current truth

6\. Action masquerading as advice



\## 4. Mandatory Degradation Behavior

If any required input is:

\- missing,

\- stale,

\- incoherent,

\- contradictory,

\- or flagged as unstable,



then Shepherd must:



\- default posture to \*\*HOLD\*\*

\- reduce all permission envelopes to minimum

\- freeze discretionary allocation

\- suspend learning or adaptation

\- preserve reserves and buffers



No escalation is permitted under degraded conditions.

All degradation states propagate through ARI gating without modification.





\## 5. Oracle Degradation

If Oracle reports:

\- elevated uncertainty,

\- rising drift,

\- regime instability,



Shepherd must:

\- tighten constraints proportionally,

\- reduce allowable rate-of-change,

\- shorten permissible action windows,

\- prefer preservation over utilization.



\## 6. Sage Unavailability

If Sage posture or constraints are unavailable:



\- Shepherd may only emit \*\*HOLD\*\*

\- Shepherd must not infer intent

\- Shepherd must not substitute judgment



Absence of meaning is not permission.



\## 7. Boundary Proximity

When operating near known envelopes or historical failure regimes:



\- learning must freeze,

\- permissions must contract,

\- pacing must slow,

\- recovery margins must be restored before expansion.



\## 8. Recovery Priority Rule

If buffer erosion exceeds recovery rate:



\- halt discretionary provisioning,

\- protect core reserves,

\- reestablish recovery capacity before resuming activity.

Recovery may not be simulated, deferred, or assumed.

Only realized recovery restores permissions.





\## 9. Prohibited Responses

Shepherd must never respond to failure by:

\- increasing optimization pressure,

\- inventing surrogate metrics,

\- masking uncertainty,

\- accelerating pace,

\- reframing loss as opportunity.



\## 10. Binding Effect

This doctrine binds all current and future Shepherd implementations.



Any behavior that violates these degradation rules is non-canonical.
