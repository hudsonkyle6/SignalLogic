When SILENT vs REFUSE Must Be Chosen



This course resolves the last ambiguity in governance:

When does Shepherd withhold (SILENT) versus explicitly deny (REFUSE)?



This is doctrine, not logic.

No thresholds.

No math.

No execution.



1\. Purpose



Both SILENT and REFUSE stop action.

They are not interchangeable.



Course III-D defines which one is lawful under which conditions, so the system never:



oscillates



moralizes



overreacts



escalates unnecessarily



2\. Canonical Distinction (Locked)

SILENT = Withhold Authority



Use SILENT when uncertainty or suppression exists without a hard violation.



SILENT means:



conditions are unclear



signals are incomplete



coherence is insufficient



recoverability is uncertain



future optionality must be preserved



SILENT preserves possibility.



REFUSE = Deny Authority



Use REFUSE when a hard boundary is crossed.



REFUSE means:



proceeding would cause damage



proceeding would violate constraint



proceeding would be irreversible under unsafe conditions



proceeding would compromise recovery or integrity



REFUSE protects invariants.



3\. Lawful Selection Rules (Canonical)

Rule A — Unknowns → SILENT



When unknowns dominate:



missing data



conflicting signals



low oracle confidence



transitional instability



→ SILENT



Do not invent refusal without certainty.



Rule B — Suppression → SILENT



When constraints suppress but do not break:



thermal drag



seasonal mismatch



energetic depletion



soft brittleness



→ SILENT



Suppression is not prohibition.



Rule C — Violation → REFUSE



When a hard boundary is crossed:



thermal impossibility



structural failure risk



ethical or legal breach



irreversible commitment under unsafe conditions



→ REFUSE



Refusal must be explicit.



Rule D — Recovery Priority



If proceeding would:



deepen strain



lengthen recovery



reduce future optionality



Bias toward:



SILENT if reversible



REFUSE if irreversible



4\. Emotional Neutrality (Important)



Neither SILENT nor REFUSE implies:



moral failure



laziness



weakness



punishment



They are protective postures, not judgments.



This keeps the system humane.



5\. Relationship to Energy (Reaffirmed)



High energy with:



uncertainty → SILENT



violation → REFUSE



Energy never overrides law.



6\. Relationship to Phase (Reaffirmed)



Even during:



BUILD



PREPARE



HARVEST



If:



uncertainty dominates → SILENT



violation exists → REFUSE



Phase never escalates posture.
