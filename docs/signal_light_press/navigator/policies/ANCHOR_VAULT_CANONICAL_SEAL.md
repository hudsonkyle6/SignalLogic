# Anchor Vault Canonical Seal

## I. Purpose

This seal designates a document as part of the Anchor Vault of Signal Light Press.

A sealed document is authoritative, referential, and protected.
It may be cited, mirrored, or interpreted, but not altered except under explicit amendment authority.

---

## II. Effect of Seal

When this seal applies:

• The document is canon  
• Its contents are considered structural law, not commentary  
• It defines boundaries, invariants, or governing truth  
• Its authority persists across implementations, refactors, and derived systems  

This seal does not grant execution rights.  
This seal does not imply readiness.  
This seal does not authorize action.

---

## III. Freeze Conditions

A canonical document is considered frozen when:

• Status is CANONICAL  
• An Effective Date is declared  
• The document resides within the Anchor Vault  
• Governance audits pass without violation  

Freeze is procedural, not symbolic.  
No footer or delimiter is required.

---

## IV. Amendment

Amendment of sealed canon:

• Must originate from Signal Light Press  
• Must declare a new Effective Date  
• Must preserve prior versions in the archive  
• Must not silently overwrite canon  

Unamended canon remains authoritative indefinitely.

---

## V. Relation to Archive

Archived documents may retain seals.

Archival status does not revoke authority.
It indicates lineage, succession, or historical placement.

Canon does not expire.
It may only be succeeded.

---

## VI. Silence Clause

If a sealed document does not speak to a condition,
it is silent by design.

Silence must not be interpreted as permission.
