PURPOSE



This directory preserves retired engines, experiments, notes, and outputs from earlier phases of the Signal system.



It exists for memory, not authority.



Nothing contained here governs present or future behavior.



CANONICAL ROLE



This archive serves three functions only:



Historical Memory

Preserve prior logic, experiments, and outcomes exactly as they existed.



Failure Containment

Prevent reactivation of known failure modes through accidental reuse.



Lineage Accountability

Ensure that lessons paid for once are not paid for again.



This archive is not a sandbox.

It is not a staging ground.

It is not a fallback system.



AUTHORITY BOUNDARIES (ABSOLUTE)



No file, code, result, or note in this directory:



has governing authority



may influence doctrine



may be imported into engines



may be cited as justification for action



may be reactivated or executed



may be modernized or “cleaned up”



Direct reuse is forbidden.



RECONSTRUCTION EXCEPTION (HUMAN-ONLY)



Material in this archive may be consulted for inspiration or historical reference under the following conditions:



Nothing is copied verbatim into active systems



Nothing is imported, executed, or linked



All extracted value is re-expressed from first principles



All responsibility rests with a human operator



Any reconstruction must:



Be explicitly approved by a human



Be re-authored under current canon



Be placed outside the archive



Be sealed with current headers and amendment rules



Be logged with source reference, date, and approver



The archive never authorizes action.

The archive never grants permission.

The archive never speaks for the present.



Reuse is forbidden.

Reconstruction is permitted.

Authority is earned anew.



INTERACTION RULES



The only lawful direction of influence is:



CANONICAL ARCHIVE  →  FAILURE TAXONOMY





The archive may inform memory of failure.

It may not inform strategy, execution, detection, or optimization.



MODIFICATION RULES



Allowed:



Explanatory comments



Contextual READMEs



Historical annotations



Failure labeling



Dating and version tagging



Forbidden:



Logic changes



Optimization



Partial reuse



Backporting



Execution



Interpretation as signal



FINAL STATEMENT



This archive is frozen history.



It is preserved so the system remembers what it survived.



It is silent by design.

It is powerful only through human restraint.
