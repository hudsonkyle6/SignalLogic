SEAL



Shepherd does not decide what to do.

Shepherd decides what may be done safely.



1\. ROLE STATEMENT (CLARITY FIRST)



Shepherd OS is the permission authority of the Signal system.



It does not:



interpret meaning (Sage)



estimate state (Oracle)



execute action (Helmsman)



remember irreversibility (Boundary Field)



Shepherd exists to ensure that no action exceeds recoverable limits, regardless of pressure, opportunity, or apparent success.



2\. AUTHORITY BOUNDARY



Shepherd is the highest authority inside the Pasture.



However:



Shepherd may not override the Forest (hard constraints)



Shepherd may not rewrite the Boundary Field



Shepherd may not bypass ARI



Shepherd may not issue commands directly to actuators



Shepherd’s outputs are permissions and limits, never instructions.



3\. WHAT “PERMISSION” MEANS (PRECISELY)



Permission is set membership, not approval.



Formally:



Shepherd defines the allowed action space

within which all downstream control must remain.



Shepherd outputs:



U\_allowed(t) — the set of permitted actions



Δu\_max(t) — maximum rate of change



Buffer\_min(t) — minimum required reserves



Posture(t) — HOLD / EASE / PROCEED



Learn\_enabled(t) — TRUE / FALSE



Nothing outside U\_allowed(t) may be attempted, optimized, or simulated.



4\. PACING — THE CORE FUNCTION



Pacing is Shepherd’s most important responsibility.



Shepherd enforces:



speed limits



step size limits



frequency limits



duty cycle limits



Even when:



signals are strong



confidence is high



opportunity appears rare



Especially then.



Success tightens pacing.

Stress tightens pacing faster.



5\. RESOURCE AUTHORITY (WHAT SHEPHERD CONTROLS)



Shepherd governs allocation, not acquisition.



Shepherd may allocate:



energy draw



capital exposure



inventory release



storage utilization



labor intensity



system attention



Shepherd may not:



create resources



borrow future capacity invisibly



hide depletion



trade recovery for speed



All allocation decisions are buffer-aware and recovery-indexed.



6\. INPUTS SHEPHERD MAY CONSIDER



Shepherd reads, but does not interpret:



From Oracle:



state estimates

From Sage:



intent framing

From Boundary Field:



envelope proximity

From Ledger:

historical recovery times



7\. HARD PROHIBITIONS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)



Shepherd is forbidden to:



Optimize for gain



Collapse uncertainty into scalar scores



Issue forward predictions



Override boundary alerts



Enable learning near edges



Trade recovery for performance



Justify risk by past success



Any violation invalidates Shepherd authority.



8\. POSTURE STATES (CANONICAL)



Shepherd operates in one of three postures only:



HOLD



buffers thinning



drift rising



uncertainty widening



learning disabled



actions minimized



EASE



recovery underway



pacing reduced



buffers rebuilding



learning limited



PROCEED



buffers healthy



margins wide



learning allowed



pacing still bounded



There is no ACCELERATE posture.



9\. LEARNING GATES (CRITICAL)



Learning is not continuous.



Shepherd explicitly controls:



learn\_enabled(t)





Rules:



learning OFF near boundaries



learning OFF under high drift



learning OFF when recovery debt exists



learning resumes only after stabilization



This prevents “learning into collapse.”



10\. RELATION TO HUMAN AUTHORITY



Shepherd preserves human responsibility by design.



Humans may:



widen permissions slowly



tighten constraints immediately



pause all action



override pacing downward



Humans may not:



silence boundary signals



erase recovery debt



delegate blame to the system



All decisions remain traceable.



11\. FAILURE MODES SHEPHERD PREVENTS



Shepherd exists specifically to prevent:



over-leverage during success



adaptation beyond recovery



invisible fatigue accumulation



optimism bias hardening



automation drift



These are the real causes of collapse.



12\. CANONICAL CLOSURE



Shepherd OS is not a strategist.

It is not a planner.

It is not an optimizer.



It is the keeper of limits.



Shepherd ensures that nothing the system does today makes tomorrow unrecoverable.
