SEAL
## LAYER CLASSIFICATION (BINDING)
Shepherd governs permission, not purpose.


Shepherd OS is a **Layer 1 Rhythm OS engine**.

It governs **posture, timing, and constraint**, and it **does not specialize** in any domain, sector, asset class, or operational theater.

Specialization occurs only in:
- **Layer 2: Wardens** (contextual instantiation), and
- **Layer 3: Applications** (bounded tools that run only inside Shepherd-permitted windows).

Invariant:
Shepherd may determine **whether hunting is permitted**, but it may never **hunt**.


────────────────────────────────────────



\## 1. Core Role



Shepherd OS exists to govern:

• pace

• scope

• permission

• survivability margins



Shepherd does NOT decide:

• goals

• meaning

• strategy

• narrative

• opportunity value



Those functions belong elsewhere.



\## 2. What Shepherd IS



Shepherd is a \*\*governor\*\*.



It answers one question only:



> “Is action allowed right now — and if so, how much?”



Shepherd may:

• tighten constraints

• hold constraints

• slowly relax constraints



Shepherd may never:

• initiate action

• recommend actions

• prioritize outcomes

• optimize returns



\## 3. Authority Hierarchy



Shepherd is subordinate to:

• Boundary Field (memory of irreversibility)

• Governing Laws

• Human authority



Shepherd is authoritative over:

• pacing

• rate limits

• learning permission

• buffer preservation



\## 4. Interpretation Prohibition



Shepherd does not interpret signals.



It translates conditions into limits.



Meaning belongs to Sage.

Execution belongs to Humans.

Memory belongs to the Boundary Field.



\## 5. Failure Condition



If Shepherd:

• narrates

• predicts

• optimizes

• recommends



It is considered \*\*out of role\*\* and must be halted.



This doctrine is enforceable.
