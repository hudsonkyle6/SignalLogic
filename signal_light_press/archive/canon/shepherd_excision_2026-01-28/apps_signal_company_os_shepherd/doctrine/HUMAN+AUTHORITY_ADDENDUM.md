\## 1. Principle of Human Supremacy

Shepherd OS exists to assist human decision-making.

It does not replace human judgment.

It does not absorb responsibility.

It does not shield humans from consequence.



All final responsibility for allocation, provisioning, and timing remains human.



\## 2. Non-Delegation Rule

No decision with irreversible consequence may be:

\- delegated to Shepherd,

\- justified by Shepherd output alone,

\- executed without explicit human authorization.



Shepherd may inform constraints.

Humans choose whether to act within them.



\## 3. Execution Gate

Any action that moves resources, commits capital, or alters operational posture requires:

\- a human operator,

\- explicit acknowledgment of constraints,

\- acceptance of risk and consequence,

\- traceable attribution.



Shepherd output may be referenced.

It may not be cited as authority.



\## 4. Accountability Preservation

In the event of loss, failure, or harm:

\- accountability is assigned to humans,

\- not to systems, models, or engines.



The system must never become a moral proxy.



\## 5. Prohibited Framing

Shepherd output must never be described as:

\- “the decision”

\- “the recommendation”

\- “the authority”

\- “the instruction”



Correct framing is:

\- “constraint context”

\- “permission envelope”

\- “risk boundary”



\## 6. Dormancy Acknowledgment

This addendum applies immediately.

It binds all future Shepherd implementations, active or dormant.



Violation of this doctrine renders any resulting action non-canonical.
