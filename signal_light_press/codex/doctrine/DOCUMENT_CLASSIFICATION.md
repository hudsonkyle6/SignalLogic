## Purpose

This document defines the official classification system for all materials governed by Signal Light Press.

Its purpose is to ensure:

- lawful intellectual property protection  
- long-term clarity and coherence  
- auditability and historical integrity  
- disciplined separation between meaning, method, record, and state  

This classification is mandatory.  
No document may exist outside these categories.

---

## CLASS I — CANON (PUBLISHED / COPYRIGHTED)

### Definition

Canon consists of works intended to preserve meaning, doctrine, shared language, and guiding principles.

These works define **what the system is**, **what it values**, and **what it refuses to do**.

### Characteristics

- No parameters  
- No thresholds  
- No algorithms  
- No execution logic  
- No sequencing instructions  

Canon explains *why*, never *how*.

### Examples

- Oracle Doctrine  
- Sage Role Doctrine  
- Posture Canon  
- Interpretation Limits  
- Memory Ethics  
- Manifestos, essays, codices  

### Legal Status

- Copyright-forward  
- Owned by Signal Light Press  
- May be selectively published or shared  

---

## CLASS II — METHODS & SYSTEMS (TRADE SECRETS)

### Definition

Methods describe **how the system actually functions**.

These materials enable operation, safety, resilience, and performance.

### Characteristics

- Fragmented by design  
- Context-dependent  
- Never complete in one place  
- Not intended for publication  

No single document may fully describe the system.

### Examples

- Threshold definitions  
- Weighting schemes  
- Decay rates  
- Coupling logic  
- Ghost damping rules  
- AC/DC conversion mechanics  
- Safety governors  
- Failure mode analyses  

### Legal Status

- Protected as trade secrets  
- Disclosure prohibited without written authorization  
- Access strictly controlled  

### Required Marking


## CLASS III — OPERATIONAL RECORDS (IMMUTABLE HISTORY)

### Definition

Operational records document **what was known**, **when it was known**, and **under what authority**.

They do not interpret, predict, or recommend.

### Characteristics

- Snapshot-bound  
- Time-stamped  
- Reproducible  
- Read-only after creation  

### Examples

- Daily Reports  
- Posture Attribution Logs  
- Keel-Laying Records  
- Frozen Oracle Snapshots  
- Audit trails  

### Legal Status

- Factual records  
- High evidentiary value  
- Not modifiable after creation  

---

## CLASS IV — EPHEMERAL STATE (DECAYING)

### Definition

Ephemeral materials support momentary coherence and operation but are not meant to persist.

They enable awareness, not authority.

### Characteristics

- Time-limited  
- Decays by policy  
- Non-authoritative  
- Never cited as canon or record  

### Examples

- Sage internal state  
- Temporary readiness indices  
- Drift metrics  
- Working notes  
- Draft reflections  

### Legal Status

- Internal use only  
- Not preserved beyond defined TTL  

---

## PROHIBITIONS

- No document may merge Canon and Methods.  
- No Method may be published as Canon.  
- No Record may be altered after creation.  
- No Ephemeral material may be treated as authoritative.  
- No retroactive justification using records or doctrine.  

---

## GOVERNING PRINCIPLE

> Publish what is meant to be remembered.  
> Protect what makes the system work.  
> Preserve records as they were.  
> Let ephemeral state fade.

This classification exists to protect truth, safety, and longevity.
