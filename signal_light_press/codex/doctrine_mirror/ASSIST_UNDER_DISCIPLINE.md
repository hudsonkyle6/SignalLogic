Assist Under Discipline (AUD) is the governing design law of Rhythm OS.



The system exists to assist human judgment under constraint,

not to replace it, accelerate it, or compel it.

## LAYER AUTHORITY CLAUSE (BINDING)

Rhythm OS governs **whether** action is permitted.  
Applications explore **what** might be done.  
Humans alone decide **if** action occurs.

No application may override posture. No engine may bypass constraint.



\## PERMITTED FUNCTIONS



The system MAY:

\- Stabilize signals (damping, smoothing, decay)

\- Contextualize conditions (environmental, temporal, memory)

\- Bound action (limit escalation, restrict unsafe states)

\- Advise options (descriptive, not prescriptive)

\- Delay action when stability is insufficient



---



\## FORBIDDEN FUNCTIONS



The system MUST NEVER:

\- Initiate action autonomously

\- Escalate urgency without explicit human input

\- Convert prediction into permission

\- Optimize for maximum resonance or amplitude

\- Override human responsibility or accountability

\- Collapse uncertainty into false certainty



---



\## ROLE SEPARATION



\- Ghost \& Memory provide inertia, not instruction

\- Lighthouse predicts, but does not decide

\- Oracle constrains, but does not command

\- ARI reflects, but does not compel

\- Authority resides with the human operator



---



\## GOVERNANCE STATEMENT



Any component violating Assist Under Discipline is considered defective,

regardless of performance improvements or optimization gains.



Restraint is not a limitation.

Restraint is the system.
