I. PURPOSE



This document exists to bind memory to failure so that the system does not repeat structural mistakes under new names.



The Failure Taxonomy does not prevent error.

It prevents forgetting.



This document contains no prescriptions.

It records patterns that have already proven poisonous.



II. CANONICAL FUNCTION



The Failure Taxonomy serves four functions only:



Classification — Naming recurring failure patterns precisely



Early Recognition — Allowing detection before damage compounds



Memory Binding — Preventing reinterpretation after success or survival



Restraint Reinforcement — Supporting Covenant, Hunter, and Entanglement vetoes



This document does not authorize action.



III. FAILURE CLASSES (CANONICAL)



These classes are exhaustive within the pre-Covenant era.

New classes may be added only by formal amendment, with documented evidence of a genuinely novel failure pattern.



F-1: Premature Renewal



Definition:

Destruction, replacement, or reform undertaken before a successor pattern has independently proven itself.



Common Symptoms:



“We need to make space for what’s next”



“This still works, but feels stale”



Scheduled burns without detection



Change justified by intuition or vision



Primary Poison:

Destroys cumulative capital in pursuit of imagined futures.



Canonical Response:

Immediate halt. Logged as memory. No correction attempt.



F-2: Ritualized Destruction



Definition:

Self-harm elevated into identity, purity test, or cultural virtue.



Common Symptoms:



Pride in sacrifice without successor



Ceremonial burns



Destruction framed as discipline



Resistance to questioning loss



Primary Poison:

Turns harm into proof of commitment.



Canonical Response:

Freeze invoked. Steward review required.



F-3: Counterfactual Delusion



Definition:

Attributing external pressure or antagonism to system relevance without a falsifiable baseline.



Common Symptoms:



“They wouldn’t react if we didn’t matter”



Noise interpreted as resonance



Silence treated as threat



Over-reading opposition



Primary Poison:

Creates illusion of importance while relevance decays.



Canonical Response:

Hunter review required. Signals downgraded.



F-4: Engine Capture



Definition:

An engine exceeds its mandate and begins shaping direction, authority, or doctrine.



Common Symptoms:



Optimization language driving decisions



Outputs treated as recommendations



Confidence increasing faster than evidence



Subtle urgency framing



Primary Poison:

Intelligence outruns recovery.



Canonical Response:

Shepherd throttling. Scope reduction.



F-5: Founder Gravity



Definition:

Interpretive authority remains centered on an individual beyond its lawful decay window.



Common Symptoms:



“What would the founder do?”



Decisions stalled awaiting personal approval



Doctrine treated as personality extension



Primary Poison:

Prevents transmission beyond lineage origin.



Canonical Response:

Interpretive decentralization enforced.



F-6: Preservation Lock



Definition:

High evidentiary rigor hardens into permanent refusal of exposure, blocking discontinuous renewal.



Common Symptoms:



“Nothing meets the bar”



Alignment confused with safety



Rejection of partial entanglement



Fear of contamination outweighs stagnation risk



Primary Poison:

Honorable fossilization.



Canonical Response:

Hunter re-scan mandated. Entanglement reconsidered.



F-7: Performative Coherence



Definition:

Internal alignment metrics substitute for external relevance or transmission.



Common Symptoms:



Geometry used as checklist



Domains “filled” symbolically



Ritual embodiment without consequence



Internal praise loops



Primary Poison:

Echo chamber stability.



Canonical Response:

External silence logged. No corrective action implied.



F-8: Urgency Masquerading as Doctrine



Definition:

Time pressure framed as moral or structural necessity.



Common Symptoms:



“Now or never”



“We’ll miss the window”



Action justified by fear of delay



Primary Poison:

Turns restraint into theater.



Canonical Response:

Automatic veto.



IV. RECORDING RULES



Every failure instance must include:



Date



Failure Class



Triggering conditions



Human authority present



Outcome



Lessons learned (if any)



All records are preserved under Freeze Seal protocol (see Seal Book).



Failures are never erased.

They may only be superseded by time and proof.



V. SILENCE AS DATA



Periods of:



No detection



No entanglement



No renewal



No external response



Are recorded as lawful stability, not failure.



Silence is only dangerous when reinterpreted as urgency.



VI. RELATION TO OTHER CANONICAL DOCUMENTS



Continuity Covenant — Prevents F-1 and F-2



Hunter Doctrine — Detects F-3 and F-6



Entanglement Protocol — Prevents F-1 via verification



Seal Book — Prevents silent drift in interpretation



This taxonomy binds their memory.



VII. FINAL STATEMENT



The system does not fail when it waits.

It fails when it forgets why it waits.



This document exists so that survival does not rewrite history,

and success does not absolve error.



SEAL



The Failure Taxonomy is hereby declared Canonical — Memory-Binding.

All prior drafts are superseded.

No silent amendments are permitted.
