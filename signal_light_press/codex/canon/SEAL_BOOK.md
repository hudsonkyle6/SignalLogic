PURPOSE



This document defines what a Seal is, what authority it carries, how it binds, and how it interacts with doctrine, freezes, and engines across The Signal Company.



The Seal Book exists to eliminate ambiguity.



From this point forward, the word SEAL is no longer symbolic.

It is a governance act.



CANONICAL DEFINITION



A Seal is a formal declaration that fixes meaning, authority, or constraint within the Signal system.



A Seal does not execute behavior.

A Seal binds interpretation, governance, or time.



Seals exist to preserve:



survivability



coherence



memory integrity



human authority



SCOPE



This Seal Book applies to:



All doctrine documents



All canonical architecture



All engine definitions (Oracle, Sage, Shepherd, Helmsman)



All freeze protocols



All registry-bound artifacts



No engine, domain, or applied system may reinterpret a Seal.



SEAL TAXONOMY (CANONICAL)



Exactly five Seal types exist.

Any document asserting binding force must explicitly declare its Seal types(s). Silence defaults to Narrative Seal only.



No others are permitted.



1\. NARRATIVE SEAL



Purpose: Declarative closure of meaning

Binding: ❌ None

Enforcement: ❌ None

Audience: Human readers



Used for:



philosophy



white papers



framing language



worldview articulation



Example:



This is intelligence as endurance.



Narrative Seals carry no authority beyond clarity.



2\. CANONICAL SEAL



Purpose: Structural truth declaration

Binding: ✅ Conceptual

Enforcement: Doctrine alignment



Used for:



role definitions



invariants



architectural truths



system boundaries



Example:



No Engine may act outside its role.



Canonical Seals bind what is true, not what is executed.



3\. GOVERNANCE SEAL



Purpose: Constraint on authority or action

Binding: ✅ Operational

Enforcement: Shepherd, ARI, contracts



Used for:



permissions



pacing rules



authority limits



learning gates



Example:



Shepherd decides what may be done safely.



Governance Seals are the highest authority inside the Pasture.



4\. FREEZE SEAL



Purpose: Temporal immutability

Binding: ✅ Absolute (time-bound)

Enforcement: Registry, archive rules



Used for:



frozen doctrine



historical snapshots



canonical versions



Example:



Status: Canonical (Frozen)



A Freeze Seal may only be lifted by Signal Light Press.



5\. EMERGENCY SEAL (RARE)



Purpose: Human override acknowledgment

Binding: Contextual

Enforcement: Human authority only

Emergency Seals do not establish precedent and may not be cited as justification for future authority or change.



Used for:



crisis documentation



manual intervention records



Emergency Seals do not create new authority.

They record that human authority was exercised.
