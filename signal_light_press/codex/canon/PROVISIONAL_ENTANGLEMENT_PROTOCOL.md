I. PURPOSE



This protocol exists to permit limited, reversible exposure to a detected successor pattern without committing the system to destruction, replacement, or renewal.



Entanglement is not adoption.

Entanglement is not endorsement.

Entanglement is not momentum.



Its sole function is verification under constraint.



II. PRECONDITION (ABSOLUTE)



Provisional Entanglement may be considered only when all of the following are true:



The Hunter Doctrine has issued a Confirmed Detection



That detection is documented and logged



The Confirmed Detection is documented under Governance Seal protocol (see Seal Book)



No other pathway can validate the successor without partial exposure



Without Confirmed Detection, entanglement is forbidden.



III. DEFINITION OF ENTANGLEMENT



Entanglement is defined as:



A time-bound, scope-limited, reversible interaction between the existing system and a detected successor pattern, conducted solely to observe whether the incumbent is obstructing a superior becoming.



Entanglement exists to answer one question only:



“Does contact reveal coherence — or collapse?”



IV. HARD CONSTRAINTS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)



All entanglement must be:



Peripheral — never core



Reversible — no irreversible commitments



Time-Bound — fixed start and end



Capital-Capped — loss fully survivable



Narrative-Neutral — no framing as progress or future



Authority-Bound — Shepherd oversight at all times



Procedural Requirement:

All constraints must be documented in advance under a Governance Seal.



If any constraint is violated, entanglement terminates immediately.



V. WHAT ENTANGLEMENT MAY DO



Provisional Entanglement may:



Expose a peripheral domain or process to the successor pattern



Allow constrained interaction under real conditions



Surface friction, incompatibility, or amplification



Reveal hidden dependencies or contradictions



Test whether obstruction is real or imagined



Entanglement exists to observe, not to improve.



VI. WHAT ENTANGLEMENT MAY NOT DO



Entanglement may never:



Replace an incumbent structure



Expand scope mid-cycle



Justify continuation through momentum



Generate urgency narratives



Create sunk-cost pressure



Modify doctrine



Alter geometry



Escalate authority



If momentum appears, it is logged as a failure signal, not success.



VII. DURATION \& TERMINATION



Every entanglement must declare in advance:



Start date



End date



Explicit termination conditions



Automatic reversion plan



Default Duration Cap:

Maximum duration is one full external rhythm cycle (seasonal or index-defined), unless doctrine explicitly specifies otherwise.



At termination:



The system must revert



No continuation is implied



No next step is assumed



Silence after termination is lawful.



VIII. POSSIBLE OUTCOMES (NO OTHERS)



Entanglement may result in only one of the following conclusions:



False Detection

The successor collapses under contact.

→ Logged. No action.



Neutral Compatibility

Coexistence without superiority.

→ Logged. No action.



Verified Obstruction

The successor demonstrates superior coherence but is blocked by the incumbent.

→ Escalates to Continuity Covenant review only — no automatic destruction or renewal.



No outcome authorizes destruction automatically.



IX. RELATION TO THE CONTINUITY COVENANT



This protocol does not weaken preservation-by-default.



It exists only to prevent false negatives when:



Detection is real



Proof requires contact



Destruction would otherwise be premature



Destruction remains illegal unless separately authorized by Covenant process.



X. FAILURE MODE (EXPLICIT)



This protocol has failed if:



Entanglement becomes habitual



Exposure is mistaken for renewal



Curiosity overrides restraint



Partial success is treated as destiny



Reversion is resisted



Failure is recorded, not hidden.



XI. FINAL STATEMENT



Provisional Entanglement is not a bridge to the future.

It is a flashlight into the present.



If nothing survives the light, nothing is lost.

If something survives the light, the system may consider stepping aside — later.



SEAL



The Provisional Entanglement Protocol is hereby declared Canonical.

All prior drafts are superseded.

No silent amendments are permitted.
