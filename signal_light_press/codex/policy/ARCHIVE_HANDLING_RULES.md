\## Purpose



These rules govern how documents are stored, accessed, modified, and preserved within the archive.



They exist to:

\- prevent drift,

\- preserve intent,

\- protect intellectual property,

\- maintain trust and auditability.



---



\## General Principles



1\. The archive is a \*\*memory system\*\*, not a workspace.

2\. What enters the archive is assumed to matter.

3\. Silence is preferable to corruption.

4\. History must remain intact, even when inconvenient.



---



\## WRITE RULES



\- Canon documents may be revised only through versioned amendments.

\- Records may be written \*\*once\*\* and never modified.

\- Methods may be appended but not consolidated into a single master document.

\- Ephemeral state must never be archived permanently.



---



\## READ RULES



\- Canon is readable by default.

\- Methods are readable only by authorized roles.

\- Records are readable but immutable.

\- Ephemeral state is readable only while valid.



---



\## DELETION \& DECAY



\- Records are never deleted.

\- Canon is never deleted.

\- Methods may be retired but preserved.

\- Ephemeral materials decay automatically per TTL policy.



---



\## FREEZING \& VERIFICATION



When a record is frozen:

\- it must be marked read-only,

\- a cryptographic hash should be generated,

\- the hash may be logged separately.



Frozen records represent \*\*historical truth\*\*, not opinion.



---



\## PROHIBITIONS



\- No retroactive edits to records.

\- No silent overwrites.

\- No reclassification without documentation.

\- No archive manipulation to justify decisions after the fact.



---



\## Stewardship Clause



Signal Light Press holds the archive in trust.



The archive exists to:

\- reduce harm caused by forgetting,

\- protect against false certainty,

\- allow future review with honesty.



The archive is not optimized for speed.

It is optimized for truth.
